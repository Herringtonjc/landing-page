Things to consider when buying

Materials

When buying tools especially, consider the material it is made of. There exists a lot of cheaply made tools on websites like Amazon. While these are not inherently bad, and I myself have purchased many items such as foam brushes and button snaps for my leather goods, stick to reputable dealers for your main tools. These might include edge bevelers, stitching chisels, and hole punches. Definitely try to avoid buying anything other than bags of leather scraps from anyone other than an actual leather company. You will find more information about some of these in the leather suppliers section at the bottom of the page.

Shipping

Another consideration is shipping times and shipping quality. This is another allure for Amazon, especially if you have a prime membership. Many of their items will ship within the day or next. Again, this is fine for non-critical tools or items you will be using. Many reputable dealers will take longer on shipping, but their tools or leather will be well worth the wait.

How to get started!

Tandy Leather

Tandy leather has some great starter tool kits for about 100USD. I started with one, and still use most of the tools today as a beginner leathercrafter! They also have some pretty good deals on leather, and have several locations around the nation. A big name in leathercrafting to be sure.

Weaver Leather Supply

Weaver is another big name in the leathercrafting world. They sponsor the videos of Corter Leather, a leathercrafter from the New England area who sells leather goods and templates, while making ASMR and tutorial videos for his YouTube. Their tools tend to be a bit more pricey, but well worth it.

OA Leather Supply

Another possibly lesser known supplier, I have never personally bought anything from them. However, their prices for leather by square footage is undeniably great. The next time I am in the market for a side of some good pull-up leather or natural veg-tan, I will absolutely be giving them a try.
